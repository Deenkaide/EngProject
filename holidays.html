Kupalle

Kupalle is a pagan holiday of the ancient Slavs. The most famous ceremonies are jumping over a fire, burning wine. In addition to the fire through which people jumped, another main fire was made. He was either on a hill or near a river. This fire was large, in the center was a huge pillar on top of which there was a wheel. One of the attributes of Kupala is wine. It was made from flowers and wild herbs.

Maslenica

Maslenica is an Eastern Slavic traditional holiday celebrated a week before Lent. Pancakes are baked on Maslenitsa and a straw man is burned. According to one version, this holiday drives away winter and calls for spring.

Kalyady

From late December to January, groups of villagers dress up in costumes and go from house to house singing traditional caroling songs. The motley cast of characters usually includes a goat, a symbol of fertility and energy, as well as a horse, an old woman, a grandfather, and a gypsy. The crowd moves from east to west, following the sun, and knocking on every door. Each visit consists of three parts: welcoming the owners of the house, performing traditional games and songs, and receiving delicious treats.
